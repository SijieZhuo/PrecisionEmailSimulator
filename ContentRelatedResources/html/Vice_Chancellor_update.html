Law School successes

Congratulations also to the Auckland Law School for their success in two recent competitions. Kelly Ding, Maria Romero de Medeiros, Andrew Fu and Ayush Sharma, with support from their coaches Celia Brownlee and Susie McCluskey, won the 2022 ICC ADC Asia-Pacific Mediation Competition.

 

Two of our students collected honours in competitions run by Te Hunga Rôia Mâori o Aotearoa Mâori Law Society. Fourth year law student Teresa Brown (Ngâpuhi, Ngâti Whâtua o Kaipara, Te Uri-o Hau, Ngâti Hine) won the Kaupapa Mâori Negotiation, and sixth year BA/LLB (Hons) student Eden Amende (Ngâpuhi, Ngâti Awa, Ngâi Tahu) won the Mâori Issues Moot and the Best Mâori Moot Group. You can read more about Teresa and Eden’s success on the News site.

Job losses at AUT

You may have seen media coverage of AUT’s announcement on Tuesday of proposed job losses and cuts to courses. As always there is speculation about whether other universities will follow suit. I cannot speak for the other universities however I can answer the question for the University of Auckland. The Voluntary Leaving Scheme (VLS) that we implemented in late 2020 made a significant contribution to our business recovery and fiscal sustainability throughout the Covid-19 pandemic. While we cannot predict future threats and challenges, we have positioned ourselves well to respond to future opportunities. We currently have no plans for any wide-scale cuts.
